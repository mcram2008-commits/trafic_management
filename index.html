📖 System Algorithms & AI Documentation

The AI Ambulance Priority Traffic Management System (APTMS) is a cutting-edge real-time traffic coordination solution. It leverages sensor fusion, Deep Learning (YOLOv8), Webster's delay calculations, Genetic Algorithms, and Reinforcement Learning (PPO) to dynamically optimize signal times and ensure immediate emergency vehicle preemption.

⚙️ Computer Vision Processing Chain
  1. Confidence Filtering: Bounding boxes with a confidence score below 0.50 are filtered out to prevent false counts.
  2. Polygon Zone Checking: Precise counting is limited to defined lanes (polygons). A ray-casting algorithm checks if the center point of a bounding box falls within the coordinates of the designated region.
  3. Queue Length Estimation: Measures vehicle density by assessing wait times and clusters of stopped objects.
APTMS uses Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) (via Stable-Baselines3 and Gymnasium) for adaptive traffic signal control.
📊 State Space (Observation)
The environment observations capture the current traffic layout:
  • Queue length per lane / approach
  • Total waiting time of vehicles
  • Current active traffic light state
🎮 Action Space
The agent determines the optimal next signal configuration:
  • KEEP GREEN: Extend the current phase (e.g., up to maximum threshold)
  • SWITCH SIGNAL: Transition to the next phase (triggers yellow light phase)
🏆 Reward Function Formulation
The reward function pushes the system to reduce congestion:
Reward = -w₁ × (Total Waiting Time) - w₂ × (Average Queue Length) - w₃ × (Emergency Delay)
*Where w₁, w₂, and w₃ are weighting parameters configured to prioritize ambulances and clear queues efficiently.
🧬 Webster's Delay Formula & Genetic Algorithm
When global optimizations are triggered, a Genetic Algorithm computes timing offsets by modeling vehicle delay.
Delay (d) = c(1-g/c)² / 2(1-x) + x² / 2q(1-x) - 0.65(c/q²)^(1/3) × x^(2+5g/c)
The GA evaluates a population of 400 configurations across 25 generations to identify the set of phase timings that yields the lowest cumulative delay.

🧠 NEAT (NeuroEvolution of Augmenting Topologies)
The NEAT coordinator simulates green waves across adjacent intersections.
  • Evolving Topology: Neurons and connections grow dynamically to learn coordination policies.
  • Green Wave Sync: Maximizes throughput on straight corridors (e.g., North-South, East-West) by offset synchronization.
  • Efficiency Gains: Yields a ~35.5% reduction in overall stop-and-go delays under peak conditions.
